Learnings

What this project taught me
about research under pressure.

Visibility is leverage

High-stakes projects carry pressure, but they also carry weight. When findings matter to the business, they move faster. The same visibility that demands precision also enables impact — if you're willing to own the findings confidently.

Seniority is about flow, not just skill

The gap between junior and senior researchers isn't just research skill. It's about navigating politics, going with the flow without losing integrity, and knowing when to hold the line or let things move. A skill I'm actively developing.

Research Process

The project changed shape —
and so did my role.

What started as concept testing shifted mid-project into iterative prototype validation. In practice it meant continuously redesigning the research approach to match where the product was, not where we'd planned to be.

Who I recruited

Sourced through Looker/Snowflake and CSMs — the top 5 revenue accounts managing multiple client teams. Required active resellers: the highest-signal group for a consolidation and billing feature.

Why concurrent methods

Concept testing and prototype validation ran at the same time. This let me probe mental models while observing real interaction — so I could immediately explore whether a breakdown was a design problem or a conceptual mismatch.

Round 01 — Concept Testing

No screens. Just conversation.

Open-ended sessions before touching a prototype. How do resellers mentally structure client relationships? What does billing ownership mean to them?

→ FindingBilling transparency is emotional, not just functional — tied to client trust and professional accountability.
Round 02 — Prototype Validation

The hierarchy clicked. The billing moment didn't.

The parent-child structure made sense quickly — but users couldn't confirm who would be charged before committing to a hierarchy change. They weren't sure who would get charged.

→ DecisionAdded explicit billing ownership confirmation before any change could be saved. Copy reframed around client impact, not account mechanics.
Round 03 — Pre-Launch Stress Test

Testing with the customers who had the most to lose.

Top-tier resellers with the most complex relationships. Sessions confirmed the revised billing flow resolved the earlier confusion. All critical issues signed off.

→ DecisionTop resellers' main concern was migration — not the feature itself. Migration support flow added to rollout; CS briefed before wider release.
